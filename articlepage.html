Salone del Mobile 2022. Trends and tendencies

One of the most obvious trends at Salone this year was the use of rich, deep hues and earthy colours.

7 min read trends

From June 7 to 12, the streets of Milan were filled with thousands of architects, designers, manufacturers, artists and artisans, all of whom came to attend the annual Design Week, explore new design innovations and exchange ideas on interior design, furniture and lighting.

Several design brands at Salone del Mobile 2022 presented their new products, which helped us understand which of them will be the main trends of the year!

Brown is the new black

One of the most obvious trends at Salone this year was the use of rich, deep hues and earthy colours. Designers opted for harmonious yet varied colour palettes, borrowing from natural hues and experimenting with their spectrum. Black, white and shades of grey were replaced by burnt brown, cream and various shades of beige, complemented by muted shades of green, blue and terracotta to create a harmonious yet sophisticated palette that subtly anchors the space.

At the same time, I would like to highlight the abundance of bright, accent colors. And the trend towards positive colors, which is most likely associated with all the events that have taken place in recent years.

Plastic, goodbye!

In keeping with the trend towards sustainability, plastics and man-made compounds were noticeably absent from the spaces presented. Instead, designers “went back to basics” and experimented with traditional materials such as wood, natural fibers (bamboo, rattan and sisal), brick, stone, marble and textiles (linen and cotton). Brass, copper and muted metallics were also used to accent pieces, along with tinted and polished glass.

Exquisite craftsmanship

The rejection of the notion of “machine production” and the emphasis on natural processes, craftsmanship and traditional construction methods were carefully explored at this year’s Salon. Whether seating, partitions, tables or accessories, the pieces were uniquely crafted using local and natural materials, paying homage to classic techniques and enhancing their individuality and authenticity.

Succulents and native trees in decor

Cacti and aloe vera were distinctively front and center in most of the interior displays. These thick, swollen plants are considered low-maintenance because they grow indoors for years, retain water in arid climates, and are often found in a variety of types and forms. Native flora such as olive trees and bamboo were also used extensively throughout the show, furthering the vernacular nod that was present throughout the exhibit designs.

Cozy luxury

Your home is your haven. A private retreat that you enjoy, that you don’t treat as a showpiece, and nothing is reserved for the best. Here, the furniture feels comfortable and inviting, and neutral tones are combined with luxury and sophistication. The trend follows a cozy, elegant line, and it’s a treat for sore eyes – we love comfort and don’t want to give it up.
